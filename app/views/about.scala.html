About




James R. Thompson, D.Phil

I'm a postdoctoral research scientist at USC in LA. I've been working at USC since March 2012 on various lipid bilayer physics projects.

Before USC I worked at Harvard University in the medical school's Systems Biology department on a nonlinear-imaging project for genetics research. Before Harvard I worked at Oxford with Dr. Mark Wallace in the Physical and Theoretical Chemistry Laboratory. I also worked in close collaboration with Prof. Hagan Bayley's group on my thesis work in the lab surrounding the assembly of the model pore-forming toxin alpha-hemolysin.

Prior to my research career, I obtained my degree in Biochemistry at the University of York, also in England.

I'm English but I live and work here in the US. In my spare time I enjoy playing music, programming, reading, photography, travelling and spending time with my friends, wife Liz and pet cat Jasper.
